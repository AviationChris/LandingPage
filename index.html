Chronicles of Fine Wines

Cabernet Sauvignon

A popular red wine known for its deep color and rich flavor. Often associated with regions like Bordeaux (France) and Napa Valley (USA).

Chardonnay

A versatile white wine that can range from crisp and mineral-like to buttery and oaked. Famous regions include Burgundy (France) and Sonoma County (USA).

Pinot Noir

A delicate and sometimes tricky red wine that has found fame in regions like Burgundy (France) and Central Otago (New Zealand).

Sauvignon Blanc

A zesty white wine known for its green and herbaceous flavors. Renowned regions include Loire Valley (France) and Marlborough (New Zealand).

Shiraz

A bold red wine that goes by Syrah in France and Shiraz in places like Australia.

Zinfandel

Known for its fruity, often spicy and robust profile, it's especially associated with California (USA).
